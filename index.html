About

I am a third year PhD student in the Computer Science Program at Vanderbilt University. My research encapsulates the interactions between perception and action in virtual and augmented environments.

My ongoing work analyzes cue saliency for navigation in augmented reality (AR). And I develop immersive simulations for medical visualization.


Research Interests

Virtual Reality, Augmented Reality, Perception, Visualization, User Interface

Camp Codette
I designed the curriculum and organized a summer coding camp for middle and high school girls. I managed a team of undergradate counselors as we instructed sessions for Python, Scratch, and Unity (game engine). Dr. Betsy Williams Sanders and I founded the camp, which was sponsored by a gift from Google Education's igniteCS program (2015-2016).
