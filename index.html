About

Haley Adams is a research scientist with a focus on perception and behavior in near-eye displays. She quantifies perceptual differences between virtual, augmented, and real environments to better understand how those differences affect the way people interact with immersive technology.

At Google, Haley leverages her expertise to inform software and hardware design for mixed reality (MR) displays. She is privileged to work alongside two of her favorite researchers: Mar Gonzalez-Franco and Björn Vlaskamp. Her work with Mar Gonzalez-Franco focuses on developing novel input solutions, while her collaboration with Björn Vlaskamp centers on spatial perception and display quality assessment.

Adams earned a PhD in Computer Science from Vanderbilt University. Her dissertation challenged the traditional assumption that photorealistic graphics were requisite for accurate spatial perception in MR displays. Further, the findings of her investigations provided evidence that non-photorealistic graphics may be useful for improving spatial perception in both normally sighted and visually impaired users.

On holiday, Haley enjoys making graphite illustrations, designing costumes, reading, and surfing. These days, she is also learning to ride a unicyle, because balance is important.

Research Interests - Virtual & Augmented Reality, Human Perception, Computer Graphics, Accessibility
